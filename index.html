Technical specifications

  • 1x Combo input (XLR or 1/4″)
  • 1x 1/4″ input (stereo right)
  • 2x 1/4″ output (stereo amp out)
  • 2x XLR output (stereo line out)
  • 1x 1/4″ send output
  • 1x 1/4″ return input
  • 1x USB-A input (MIDI)
  • 1x 5V USB-C (power supply)
  • 1x USB-C (interface/control)
  • 6.25″ touchscreen (720×1560)
  • Raspberry CM5

WizeCore FAQ

Can I connect WizeCore to other devices?

Yes. Although WizeCore works as a standalone unit, it can also connect seamlessly to phones, tablets, and desktops. It integrates as a VST plugin within your DAW and includes built in WiFi and Bluetooth for wireless control, updates, and communication.

Can I plug a microphone directly into it?

Yes.

The combo input supports XLR microphones and 1/4″ instrument cables. The XLR mic input is balanced, which helps reduce noise and interference during live use or recording.

Does WizeCore support stereo input and output?

Yes.

WizeCore supports stereo signal routing with independent left and right connections for both input and output. You can run stereo rigs, dual amp setups, synths, backing tracks, or complex effect chains without needing external routing hardware.

What instruments work with WizeCore?

Pretty much anything you can throw at it.

Guitar, bass, synths, MIDI controllers, drum machines, vocals, experimental setups, weird homemade instruments connected with too many cables…

We respect all of them equally.

Does it support external effects pedals?

Yes.

WizeCore includes a dedicated send/return loop, so you can integrate external analog pedals directly into your signal chain.

That means you can combine plugins, digital processing, and your favorite physical pedals in the same setup.

What are Analog FX Cards?

Analog FX Cards are swappable hardware effect modules that plug directly into WizeCore.

The first release includes an analog distortion card, giving you real analog gain inside the signal chain.

More card types may come in the future, allowing users to swap and expand their setup without replacing the whole device.

Can I use it for live performance?

That’s exactly what it’s built for.

The idea was never to replace creativity with menus and tiny screens. It’s meant to give you the flexibility of software in a format that actually belongs on a stage.

Where can I get new plugins?

You can download plugins directly from our curated store, or install additional ones from popular LV2 plugin sources such as:

  • MOD Audio
  • Patchstorage
  • KXStudio
When can I buy it?

WizeCore is expected to be available in a limited early sale batch starting in June 2026, with full production planned for late 2027.

Can I use MIDI devices?

Yes.

WizeCore supports MIDI connectivity through USB, allowing you to connect MIDI controllers, keyboards, sequencers, and other compatible devices.

Can it work as an audio interface?

Yes.

The USB-C interface/control connection allows integration with computers and external software for audio workflows, control, and future software expansion.

What is the user interface like?

WizeCore features an integrated touchscreen for direct control, along with three footswitches for hands free operation, ideal for live performance and quick preset switching.

What computer powers the system?

WizeCore runs on a Raspberry Pi CM5.

That gives it the flexibility of a compact computer inside a stage ready hardware format.

Basically:

computer brain, pedal body.

Is it powered through USB-C?

Yes.

WizeCore uses a 5V USB-C power input.

Are the outputs balanced?

The XLR outputs are balanced.

This is useful for cleaner signal transmission in studio environments and live stages with longer cable runs.

Which plugins can run on WizeCore?

WizeCore supports any plugin compatible with Raspberry Pi architecture (ARM based systems). In general, this includes LV2 plugins designed for Linux based environments.

Are the plugins free?

Many plugins are open source and free to use. In addition, premium plugins from third party vendors will also be available through the store.

You will also be able to publish and sell your own plugins through the WizeCore store.

How are updates handled?

System and plugin updates are delivered via WiFi, allowing you to easily keep your device up to date with new features, improvements, and security fixes.

How much will it cost?

Pricing is still being finalized to ensure the best balance between component quality and affordability. The expected range is between €800 and €1100.

What type of enclosure does it have?

The enclosure is made of aluminum, providing excellent heat management, durability, and a premium look.

Can I record audio with WizeCore?

Yes. WizeCore includes a built in looper and a micro DAW, allowing you to record and experiment directly on the device.

Is it open source?

Not fully. However, parts of the software and plugin ecosystem will be made available to the community.
